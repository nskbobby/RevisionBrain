Facts about Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

  • Definition: The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a systematic process for planning, creating, testing, and deploying software. It helps ensure that software meets quality standards and user requirements.
  • Phases: Common phases in SDLC include:
    • Planning
    • Requirements Analysis
    • Design
    • Implementation
    • Testing
    • Deployment
    • Maintenance
  • Models: There are several models of SDLC, including:
    • Waterfall Model
    • Agile Model
    • Iterative Model
    • Spiral Model
    • V-Model
  • Benefits: Implementing SDLC helps:
    • Enhance project management and efficiency.
    • Improve communication between stakeholders.
    • Ensure quality and consistency in software products.
    • Facilitate better planning and risk management.
  • Agile Methodology: Agile is a popular SDLC model that emphasizes iterative development, collaboration, and flexibility to change, allowing teams to adapt to evolving project requirements.
  • Quality Assurance: QA activities are integrated throughout the SDLC to identify and fix defects early, reducing costs and enhancing product quality.
